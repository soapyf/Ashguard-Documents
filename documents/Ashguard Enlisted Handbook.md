# Welcome to the Ashguard

Welcome to our community! As you may already know, we are an Elder Scrolls-inspired group dedicated to creativity, engaging activities, and participating in SLMC "Linden Damage" combat.

This handbook serves as your guide to the group's rules and your conduct within it. These guidelines are based on common sense and should hold no surprises.

Additionally, this handbook provides information about the group's structure, so you can understand what to expect and how things work. You can always refer back to it by visiting [https://handbook.ashguard.net](https://handbook.ashguard.net). If this is your first time reading it as a new recruit, your trainer will likely be reading through this information with you. For any future questions, feel free to reach out to your trainer, who will be more than happy to assist you. If they're unavailable, any other group or temple member will be happy to assist.

---

## Code of Conduct

This section outlines the rules and standards you are expected to follow as a member of Ashguard. Given the nature and history of our community, this handbook begins with clear and firm prohibitions. We know these values are shared broadly across the community. Still, as with any competitive environment, it's best to clearly lay out expectations.

### Zero-Tolerance Policy

There are several behaviors deemed unacceptable by The Ashguard and will not be tolerated:

- **Doxxing:** Sharing real-life information about other users, including pictures, private details, or links to their social media accounts.
- **Malicious Actions:** Using tools to crash a user's client or computer, or to crash or grief sims.
- **Content Theft:** Stealing or copybotting content that does not belong to you, whether inside or outside of Second Life, or distributing group assets without express permission.
- **Abuse of Reports:** Misusing Linden Labs' abuse report system to get another user's account suspended or banned from Second Life without just cause.

Violating any of these rules may result in immediate removal from Ashguard and may lead to exclusion from the wider community. Ashguard supports the Big 4 initiative and will comply with any blacklisting decisions made by our community partners following an appropriate investigation.

### Group Policy

In Ashguard, our focus is on community. We aim to create a relaxed and casual environment while also embracing a competitive spirit when working towards our goals. As a result, we have established ourselves as a key pillar in the SLMC, known for both our strong skills and our laid-back nature, which has earned us a positive reputation within the broader community. To that end, we've established a few group-wide policies beyond the zero-tolerance rules above:

- Members of Ashguard should conduct themselves in a way that reflects positively on both themselves and the group. While we all enjoy a bit of mischief — and are indeed known for it — our mischief reflects a friendly, open spirit. We treat people well, and they will reciprocate in kind.

- During our primary SLMC activity, known as "raiding" (where our group visits another group's sim in order to engage in combat with them), we generally maintain a relaxed communications policy. However, if a raid becomes intense or if we need to focus on competitive objectives, a "raid comms" policy may be implemented. In such cases, the officer in command (referred to as the OIC) may request that we "clear comms" to ensure important information can be communicated clearly and effectively. Our group uses an in-world comms feature integrated into our HUD, which your trainer will introduce you to. This feature helps reduce communication congestion during raids and is also useful if you prefer not to speak.

- Within Ashguard, there is a chain of command, which is described in more detail later in this handbook. However, this structure is not as rigid as it may seem and is primarily used for light role-playing or to reflect individuals' time in the group and their day-to-day responsibilities. Rank authority is not used against you, except in maintaining the group's standards and rules. If someone asks you to do laps of the manor district, it's meant to be a bit of fun, not a serious command. Additionally, during raid engagements, the only authority that matters is the Officer in Charge (OIC), as previously outlined. The OIC has full control over the raid, and their decisions take precedence over other ranks within the group structure. This helps streamline the event and ensures effective management.

### Uniform and Combat Avatar Policy

In Ashguard, as with all SLMCs and similar groups on role-play sims, a uniform is expected when you are "on duty," which aligns with being "in character."

After completing your training and receiving your bonemold armor, you'll have the opportunity to personalize it. This armor is highly moddable, and we encourage you to create a unique Ashguard character that reflects your individuality. You may have seen various takes on the armor featured on our hub or website, and we're eager to see your personal style shine through.

For combat and excursions, all members are required to maintain a dedicated combat-ready avatar, your "Ashguardian." Full details on building your combat avatar, including presentation standards, build cleanliness requirements, and the approval process, can be found in the Ashguard Combat Avatar Policy. All members should review and comply with that policy.

As a general guide:

- We are a fantasy-themed sim set in the Elder Scrolls universe. When considering equipment and materials, keep in mind the setting's aesthetic and lore. Avoid cybernetics or technologies that wouldn't fit within this universe. For body enhancements, think along the lines of crystal-powered Dwemer-style gear or bound magicka adornments and appendages.
- While on duty, Enlisted Personnel and above must primarily wear bonemold armor. This helps us identify each other during conflicts and is a crucial part of our group identity. As a soldier of Resdayn, you also serve as a city guard, so your attire should reflect this role.
- Your combat avatar must be combat-appropriate and non-sexual in nature. Keep your build clean by removing unnecessary scripts, particle effects, and non-combat attachments. See the Combat Avatar Policy for full details.

### Off Duty in Resdayn

You are allowed to enter an off-duty state while on the sim if you wish to relax in the Headquarters or Temple. During these times, you can wear theme-appropriate apparel instead of your full armor. Many Ashguard members choose to wear partial armor to indicate their casual state.

If you wish to go off duty, the group "Ashguard - Civilians" is appropriate for this purpose. However, if an attack occurs, it is advisable to don your armor and prepare for action!

### Conduct During Combat

During raids, there are specific expectations regarding communication and equipment use. Raids typically begin with a phase called "low escalation," where only basic equipment is used. As the raid progresses, the level of equipment may escalate, as directed by your Officer in Command (OIC). It is also standard practice that during any raid, you may only use equipment issued by Ashguard. Your combat avatar must comply with the Ashguard Combat Avatar Policy.

Below are some general rules regarding combat:

- Communications with opposing factions should be kept between OICs. Do not message opposing members if you have issues with their gameplay or equipment. Instead, you should bring it up with your OIC.
- During combat and raids, the OIC is considered law, and their decisions take precedence over other ranks to ensure a unified and effective response.
- If you have an issue, bring it to the attention of the OIC. Some issues cannot be handled until after a raid is over, so please be patient with the OIC.
- Do not use any gear or equipment that has not been authorized. The OIC of the raid will indicate what can and can't be used. If in doubt, you may ask. Remember, if it is a busy raid, to utilize your /c comms.

**Discord** is our primary communication platform, and as such there are some guidelines to be aware of:

- You should endeavor to be in a voice channel while participating in combat. You do not need to speak, but it is strongly recommended that you are able to listen.
- It is a big help if your nickname on the Ashguard Discord is your in-world name, especially while people learn voices. As a general rule, we require this.
- Comms should be kept tame during raids. We love a good conversation during raids and oftentimes the banter does escalate. However, you should be prepared to "clear comms" in the event of OIC communication.
- Disruptive noises and memes should be kept toned down during combat, as many members of Ashguard are neurodivergent and as an inclusive community we want everyone to feel comfortable. During raids this is important as a lot is always going on. Please be considerate.

### Activity / Inactivity Policy

In Ashguard, we understand that Second Life is not a priority and that life, health, and interests can take you in different directions. However, as an active community, we rely on member participation, and maintaining an active membership is important to the health of our group.

If you plan to be inactive for a considerable length of time (typically beyond a few weeks), we appreciate it if you let us know. This is known as a Leave of Absence (LOA) in our community. Despite the formal name, it's a simple process, and you can request one for any reason. We just like to stay informed about where everyone is.

We do have a process for handling inactivity:

- If you are inactive for one month without notifying anyone, a member of the Temple will reach out to check in. If you inform them that you're on an LOA, they'll update the group, and you'll be all set.
- If there is no response from you and you remain inactive for another month, someone will reach out again before your enlistment in the group is ended.

This is not the end, however. You are welcome to return in the future and go through an expedited refresher training cadre, where we'll ensure you're updated on the same information you received the first time and get you all set on what is new in the group. Your equipment, unlocks, and group motes will all be saved, but you will restart at the rank of E1.

### Civilians and Visitors

Civilians in Resdayn are Ashguard-aligned citizens of Resdayn. This is a social role for those who wish to hang out in Resdayn, role-play, or simply relax without participating in combat. As a member of Ashguard, part of your role is to protect civilians and be welcoming to visitors. We value our guests and always encourage more people to come and experience our community!

If a visitor is interested in becoming a registered civilian or soldier of the Ashguard, you can direct them to the nearest member of the Temple. If you cannot find a member of the Temple, direct them to join via the following link: [https://join.ashguard.net/](https://join.ashguard.net/).

## Group Structure

As a new member of Ashguard, you will begin at the rank of T-1 – Trainee, where you'll undergo training and become familiar with the group's structure and expectations. Upon completion, you'll be promoted to E-1 – Retainer | Yivid, marking the official start of your service within Ashguard.

While ranks indicate your responsibilities and access within the group, they are not a measure of your personal worth. Progression in Ashguard is not solely about advancement — it's about contributing to the community in ways that suit your interests and strengths. Below is a full list of ranks, including their titles in both Standard Imperial and Dunmeri.

### Militia

Hireling | Hagil is the rank held by members of the community that wish to fight for us but do not want to join the group. This is an informal role open to anyone who wishes to fight for Ashguard without committing to full membership. Benefits and advancement remain limited until the individual chooses to join through our standard application and training process.

They share a group with the Trainees, but do not gain access to the specialized training equipment and mentorship.

### Enlisted

The majority of the group serves within the Enlisted ranks, which span E-1 through E-9. Many members choose to remain within these ranks long-term, and you will find that a significant portion of your comrades sit comfortably in the mid-Enlisted range.

Your training doesn't end once you reach E-1; you'll gain access to new equipment and continue learning through active participation in combat with the group. Your mentor will always be available if you need them, but anyone in the group will be more than happy to assist you with any questions.

In addition to equipment and training, Enlisted members of Ashguard may also be afforded the privilege of a house in our current capital (Balmora). This land is granted as a mark of trust within the group. Allocation and use of such land remain subject to Ashguard oversight and expectations of conduct.

This rank is reserved for members who have consistently gone above and beyond in their service to Ashguard, demonstrating reliability, competence, and sustained commitment. NCOs are typically responsible for enforcing group standards and sim behavior guidelines.

Officers in Ashguard primarily serve administrative roles and are responsible for overseeing the day-to-day operations of the group. They manage most of the group's funding and typically hold a Canton role within the organization.

Officers have access to all equipment in the group, so if you ever need information, a demonstration of advanced gear, or advice on the future direction of the group, they are the people to ask. Officers are here to support you as a member and are always approachable.

Cantons are unranked secondary roles available to any member of Ashguard who has reached the rank of Enlisted. These roles are intended for those who wish to contribute further to the group beyond regular combat. Choosing a Canton is optional and not required for progression.

## Currency — Motes of Knowledge (Motes)

Within Ashguard, participating in activities such as combat and being active on our sim will earn you "Motes of Knowledge." These motes can be used to acquire new equipment, such as weapons or cosmetics, within the group. This is your base-level primary resource and will no doubt be used for more advanced resources.

Your motes will be displayed on your HUD, allowing you to track your balance, and your mentor will guide you on how to spend them. Given our high level of activity and diverse engagements, you'll accumulate motes quickly!
